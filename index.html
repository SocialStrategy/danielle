Content Creation Process

Document vs. Create Approach

Following Gary Vee's document vs. create philosophy, Danielle should capture authentic moments from her practice and daily routines to share genuine insights with her audience:

  • Morning Ritual Documentation: Capture brief moments from personal wellness practices (tea preparation, meditation, etc.)
  • Client Session Insights: Document general patterns and insights from client work (maintaining confidentiality)
  • Herbal Preparation Process: Show the authentic process of working with herbs and formulations
  • Astrological Chart Work: Document the process of analyzing charts and finding patterns
  • Learning Moments: Share insights from continuing education and research

Content Calendar Structure

  • Monthly Themes: Align with seasonal energies and astrological events
  • Weekly Focus: Rotate through content pillars to maintain variety
  • Daily Mix: Balance educational, inspirational, behind-the-scenes, and community content
  • Batch Creation: Set aside dedicated time for content creation (2-4 hours weekly)
  • Content Bank: Maintain a library of evergreen content for consistent posting

Metrics & Analytics

Key Performance Indicators

Track these metrics to measure strategy effectiveness and guide optimization:

Growth Metrics

  • Follower Growth Rate: Monthly percentage increase in followers
  • Reach: Number of unique accounts that see your content

  • Engagement Rate: Interactions divided by reach or followers

  • Click-through Rate: Percentage of viewers who click links

Conversion Metrics

  • Consultation Bookings: Appointments attributed to social media
  • Service Inquiries: Questions about offerings from social followers
  • Content Downloads: Resources accessed from social links

Analytics Framework

Tracking System

  • Weekly Quick Review: Brief check of key metrics (15 minutes)
  • Monthly Deep Dive: Comprehensive analysis of all metrics (60 minutes)
  • Quarterly Strategy Review: Evaluate overall performance and adjust strategy (90 minutes)

Content Performance Analysis

  • Time Investment: Track hours spent on social media activities
  • Financial Investment: Any costs for tools, assistance, or paid promotion
